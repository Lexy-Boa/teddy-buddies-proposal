Before the numbers

A Personal Note Before the Numbers

Teddy Buddies is family. I hold a small shareholding and the school's growth is, for me, a personal matter before it is a professional one. None of that goes away when we sign a services agreement.

What is changing is that the scope of what I am doing for Teddy Buddies has expanded well beyond the original ₹10,000/month social-media oversight. The AI department buildout, the embedded operational involvement, the franchising and SOP groundwork, the strategic advisory through the daily standups, and the marketing + board IG/LinkedIn growth — all of it belongs inside a properly structured engagement between Teddy Buddies and my consultancy, AIBC Foundry.

Following the board discussion, the structure has been cleaned up so the two streams are clearly separated: my cash remuneration, and the AI department's subscription costs. They are now billed separately, to different accounts — that section spells out exactly how.

If anything in here feels off — too much, too little, wrong structure — I would rather rework it than have unspoken concerns sit in the background.

02 — Background and Context

Where Teddy Buddies stands

Teddy Buddies Schools currently has ~200-students. The business is positioned for growth — including franchising and multi-unit expansion — and is in the early stages of operationalising an internal AI department to support administration, parent communications, content generation, and the scale-up of future units.

My involvement to date has spanned three overlapping streams:

  1. Technology / AI — design and rollout of the Teddy Buddies AI Department, build on the OpenClaw + FastAPI + Claude API architecture that AIBC Foundry is productising for Indian SMEs. Internal workflow automations (e.g. daily analytics reports to manage marketing spends actively) and an SOP agent (as recommended by the consultants) are in the pipeline.
  2. Business Development — Content team development for growth and involvement in marketing team, expansion, and franchising readiness; growing the board members' personal presence on Instagram and LinkedIn.
  3. Embedded Operations — daily 3 PM standup, day-to-day operational input, marketing and social media oversight, and ad-hoc decision support.

This proposal formalises the full scope under a single retainer.

03 — Scope of Engagement

What the retainer covers

Rather than carve scope into rigid line items — which does not fit either the spirit of a family business or the realities of running an SME — AIBC Foundry will provide an integrated retainer covering operational, technical, and strategic involvement at Teddy Buddies Schools. In practice this includes:

Technology & AI Department

  • Architecture, deployment, and ongoing operation of the Teddy Buddies AI infrastructure (OpenClaw messaging layer, FastAPI application logic, Postgres data layer, Claude API reasoning layer, hosted on a dedicated VPS).
  • AI-driven parent communications on WhatsApp, including admissions enquiries, schedule queries, fee reminders, and routine information requests.
  • Internal automation for school administration (records, schedules, reports).
  • Content generation pipelines for marketing, social media, and curriculum-adjacent material.
  • Staff orientation and training (in a slow and easy manner) so the team can operate the AI department day-to-day.

Business Development

  • SOP development for the core operating processes of a Teddy Buddies unit, structured for replication.
  • Franchise-readiness package: process inventory, documentation, validation by an unfamiliar tester, and any other wide scope requirements as and when they arise.
  • Growth and expansion advisory at founder level.

Embedded Operations

  • Daily 3 PM standup attendance and ongoing operational involvement.
  • Marketing and digital oversight, including growing the board members' personal presence on Instagram and LinkedIn (continuation and extension of existing scope).
  • Ad-hoc strategic input on decisions as they arise.
04 — Commercial Terms

How the pricing is built

4.1  Pricing Logic

The AIBC Foundry standard rate for this scope of work — full-stack AI department buildout combined with strategic advisory, board-level digital-presence growth, and embedded operational involvement — is ₹1,10,000 per month. This rate is benchmarked against similarly comparable consulting and AI implementation solopreneurs in Bangalore and Trivandrum, where:

  • AI department buildouts (architecture + deployment + integration + training) are typically quoted at ₹2.5–5 lakh as one-time projects.
  • Ongoing AI operations + strategic advisory + embedded operational involvement, bundled, typically starts between ₹90,000 and ₹1,40,000 per month for SMEs of Teddy Buddies' size and revenue profile.

4.2  Two Clean Streams — Cash Retainer + AI Subscriptions

Following the board's request for an explicit split between my remuneration and the AI department's costs, the engagement is structured as two distinct streams, billed to two distinct accounts:

4.3  AI Department Subscriptions — Paid Directly by Teddy Buddies

Jachin is the AI Head of the Teddy Buddies AI Department. The subscriptions below power that department's work — automation, content generation, parent communications, content production for TB social channels, model evaluations, and capability testing. They are Teddy Buddies' operating cost, not Jachin's personal cost, and they are billed accordingly:

Anthropic Claude Max ₹12,500
OpenAI / ChatGPT ₹2,000
Google Gemini ₹1,000
Captions.ai ₹1,000
Higgsfield AI ₹4,700
Total — paid directly by TB ₹21,200 / month

How this is billed: Jachin will link each of the above subscription accounts directly to Teddy Buddies' company card / bank account. The vendors charge TB; TB sees the invoices on its own books; no amount of this ₹21,200 is routed via AIBC Foundry, and none of it is paid to Jachin in cash, ever. Jachin's only cash income from TB is the ₹40,000 retainer.

Because Jachin operates these subscriptions as the AI Head, capability is also available to TB across the full department's surface area — including testing new AI upgrades before purchasing them in seat numbers for the wider staff, and content production for TB's social presence.

4.4  Out of Scope Work

The retainer is intentionally broad and trust-based. There is no hourly billing and no scope-creep enforcement. We are family and we are in this together. Anything I can help with for Teddy Buddies to grow will be taken into consideration, analysed, discussed, built and enforced as and when the need arises.

4.5  Review and Escalation

  • Quarterly formal review: Both parties revisit scope and share feedback. Either side can propose changes.
  • Monthly informal check-in: Short conversation to flag anything that feels miscalibrated before it festers. Goal checks and alignment discussions.
  • Annual escalation: the list price escalates 10% per year (or is renegotiated at review); the family/founder discount is revisited in good faith so the net retainer stays fair to both sides.

05 — Term and Termination

Term and Termination

  • Start date: 1 June 2026.
  • Term: Open-ended engagement.
  • Notice period: 60 days from either side, in writing.
06 — Intellectual Property

Intellectual Property

  • AIBC Foundry retains ownership of: the OpenClaw deployment template, the FastAPI + Postgres + Claude API agent architecture, and all reusable consulting artefacts. Teddy Buddies receives a perpetual, royalty-free licence to use these for its own operations.
  • Teddy Buddies retains ownership of: all TB-specific configurations, content, brand assets, student/parent data, franchise documentation customised for TB, and any IP developed specifically for TB within and outside the retainer scope.
  • Data handling: All parent, child, and staff data remains the property of Teddy Buddies. AIBC Foundry will not use TB data for training, demonstrations, or external case studies without prior written consent from TB.
07 — Payment Terms

Payment Terms

  • Cash retainer — ₹40,000 / month: Bank transfer to Jachin Jose Victor, monthly in advance by the 6th of each calendar month. This is the entire cash remuneration for the engagement.
  • AI Department subscriptions — ₹21,200 / month: Charged directly to Teddy Buddies' company card / bank account. Each subscription is linked to TB's billing instrument at the vendor; TB receives the invoice; no reimbursement loop, no cash to an individual.
  • What never happens: Cash transfers to Jachin for tooling. Bundled invoices that mix consulting fees and subscription costs. Reimbursement claims for AI subscriptions. The two streams stay separate, on different accounts, every month.

If any term needs adjustment — pricing, scope, structure, or anything else — let's talk through it before finalising. The numbers and structure here are AIBC Foundry's recommendation, not a take-it-or-leave-it.
